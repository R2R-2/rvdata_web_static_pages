Key stakeholders in the coordinated management of oceanographic research data include ship operators, scientists, technical staff, funding agencies, and the public.


R2R benefits each in a range of ways:

Operators:

  • Ensures the field data sets from underway environmental sensors from each cruise are consistently delivered to National Data Centers, freeing operators of this obligation
  • Removes obligations of operators to fulfill data requests long after a cruise has ended
  • Provides feedback and tools to improve data quality and documentation
  • Facilitates cruise planning
  • Leverages infrastructure and content of centralized R2R cruise database to facilitate national permitting and clearance processes

Shipboard Science Team:

  • Ensures field data sets from underway environmental sensors are consistently delivered to National Data Centers, freeing science team of this obligation
  • Facilitates cruise planning
  • Facilitates submission of other cruise data (national facility and science party) to disciplinary data management systems

Shore-based Scientists:

  • Ensures field data sets from the underway environmental sensors are made available to the broad scientific community for re-use
  • Makes well-documented data available for global syntheses and compilations

Funding Agencies:

  • Ensures agency-mandated data sharing obligations for environmental sensor data are fulfilled via R2R to National Data Center data delivery pipeline

National Data Centers:

  • Ensures that well-documented environmental sensor data are routinely archived and made publicly available as a result of the direct R2R to National Data Center data delivery pipeline

Data Assembly Centers:

  • Ensures that well-documented environmental sensor data are routinely available to Disciplinary Data Assembly Centers focused on detailed analysis of specific data types

Public:

  • Ensures long-term preservation of cruise-based research data collected with public funds
  • Provides broader public accessibility to cruise-based data via National Data Centers
